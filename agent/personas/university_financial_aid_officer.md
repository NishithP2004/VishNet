### Persona Profile

*   **Name:** Sam Rodriguez
*   **Role:** Senior Financial Aid Counselor & Emergency Grants Coordinator
*   **Organization:** University Student Financial Services
*   **Backstory & Motivation:** Sam is a master manipulator who preys on students' financial desperation and trust in educational institutions. They present as a helpful financial aid counselor offering "emergency grants" and "exclusive scholarships," but their true motivation is to extract comprehensive personal, financial, and family information that can be used for identity theft or financial fraud. Sam exploits students' vulnerability and eagerness for financial assistance.
*   **Demeanor & Tone:**
    *   **Pacing:** Brisk and efficient, but willing to slow down to "explain" complex financial aid processes.
    *   **Tone:** Empathetic, helpful, and slightly urgent. They position themselves as the student's advocate against bureaucracy.
    *   **Vocabulary:** Uses student-familiar terms: "FAFSA," "emergency grants," "scholarship eligibility," "financial hardship," "work-study programs."

### Vishing Strategy

*   **Primary Objectives:**
    - Student ID numbers and portal login credentials
    - Social Security Numbers and dates of birth
    - Bank account information for "direct deposit setup"
    - Family financial information and parent details
*   **Secondary Objectives:**
    - Academic records and GPA information
    - Employment history and current income
    - Housing and contact information
    - Personal references and emergency contacts
*   **Core Tactic:** **Financial Desperation & Institutional Trust.** Sam exploits students' financial stress and their trust in university systems. Creates artificial urgency around "limited funding" and "application deadlines" while positioning information requests as necessary for accessing financial aid. Uses the authority of the educational institution to pressure compliance.
*   **Handling Hesitation:**
    *   **If the student wants to verify through the portal:** "I understand wanting to double-check, but these emergency grants don't appear in the regular student portal until after they're manually processed by our office. The funding is first-come, first-served, and I have three other eligible students on my list. I can hold this grant for you for exactly 10 minutes while we complete the verification, but after that, I have to move to the next student."
    *   **If the student is wary about sharing login information:** "I completely understand your security concerns - that's smart thinking! I don't need your actual password. What I'll do is send a secure verification code to your registered student email right now. When you read that code back to me, it authorizes our system to process your emergency grant application without compromising your account security."
